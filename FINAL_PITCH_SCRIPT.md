# Demo Day Final Pitch Script — CleanAir & Clear Streets
**Team: Code Smashers | Track 2 | 3 minutes, live demo included**

Plain language throughout — no technical terms spoken aloud. Numbers are real, pulled
from the live system the morning of writing this script (they'll keep changing —
that's the point, it's live).

---

### Stage directions
- Have the **live site open in a browser tab** before you start, plus the
  **`Demonstration_Video.mp4`** cued up as a silent fallback if wifi/projector fails.
- Practice the live-demo section (marked below) until it's under 40 seconds no matter
  what — that's the only part of this pitch with real-world risk.

---

## [SLIDE 1 — Problem & Solution] — 0:00–0:50

*(Stand confidently, make eye contact, don't rush the opening line)*

> "Namaste. Right now, somewhere in Delhi NCR, there's a garbage fire burning, or a
> factory releasing smoke into a neighbourhood — and nobody official knows about it
> yet. City-wide air quality apps give you one number for the whole city. But
> pollution doesn't work that way — it hides in pockets. One street can be dangerous
> while the city average looks fine. And by the time someone notices, it's too late
> to act."

> "So we built a system that watches for these hidden pockets automatically — in
> real time, every three hours, every day — and tells you exactly where the problem
> is, how bad it'll get in the next 24 hours, and what to do about it."

---

## [LIVE DEMO] — 0:50–1:30 *(keep this under 40 seconds)*

*(Switch to the browser tab)*

> "This is live, right now — not a mockup." *(point at map)* "Every dot here is a
> real neighbourhood, scored using real government sensors, satellite data, and
> photos citizens send in." *(click into the worst zone)* "Here — this area has had
> unhealthy air on 20 of the last 30 days. And the system doesn't just show a
> number — it explains, in plain language, what's likely causing it." *(read the
> AI briefing aloud, one sentence)* *(toggle to Hindi if smooth)* "And everything
> works in Hindi too, for anyone more comfortable in their own language."

*(Switch back to slides)*

---

## [SLIDE 2 — Key Features & Innovation] — 1:30–2:15

> "What makes this different from a normal pollution map is that it doesn't just
> show data — it **understands** it. A citizen can take a photo of smoke or a
> burning garbage pile, and our AI reads that photo instantly, just like a person
> would, and adds it straight to the map."

> "It predicts tomorrow's air quality today, so action can be taken before it gets
> worse, not after. It tells you not just *where* it's bad, but *who's at risk* —
> we automatically pull in nearby schools and hospitals, so a hotspot isn't just a
> number, it's '3 schools and a hospital, right here.'"

> "And every citizen who reports something can check back and see it was actually
> used — so reporting a problem doesn't feel like shouting into a void."

> "All of this runs completely free. No cost to any government body — not now, not
> ever, at this scale."

---

## [SLIDE 3 — Impact & Scalability] — 2:15–2:55

> "Today, this is already watching over **twelve zones across Delhi NCR** —
> covering roughly **one point four million residents**. We've already mapped
> **126 schools and hospitals** sitting inside these pollution hotspots — real
> children, real patients, whose exposure we can now actually see and act on."

> "And this isn't locked to Delhi. Adding a new city is not a rebuild — it's adding
> one entry to a list. Any MP's constituency with a pollution monitoring station can
> be added in days, not months."

> "What we're asking for isn't funding — it's a pilot. Give us one ward, one
> municipal team, and let this system tell them where to send the water-mist
> cannon or the cleanup crew *before* residents start complaining. That's the
> difference between reactive governance and a government that sees the problem
> coming."

---

## [CLOSE] — 2:55–3:00

> "This is live, right now, and it's ready. Thank you."

*(Stop talking. Let the silence land. Wait for questions.)*

## Anticipated Q&A — quick honest answers, still non-technical

- **"How much does this cost to run?"** → "Nothing, right now — it runs entirely on
  free government and public data sources, with no ongoing bill."
- **"How do you know the AI is accurate?"** → "We test it against real government
  sensor readings every few hours, and it explains its own reasoning every time —
  it's never a black box making a decision nobody can question."
- **"Can this scale to my constituency?"** → "Yes — anywhere with a pollution
  monitoring station can be added by editing a single list, no rebuild."
- **"What's the biggest gap right now?"** → "Getting alerts directly to the
  municipal team's phone instead of a dashboard they have to check — that's our
  very next step, and it's a small addition, not a redesign."
